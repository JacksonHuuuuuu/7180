General Guidance

--- CAMPING GUIDENCE ---

For you to make a unique travel plan

Forest Camping

      Campsite Popularity ➔ ★ ★ ★ ★
      ◈ Use Insect Protection

       Protect yourself from mosquitos, ticks and other insects, use insect repellent. It’s recommended to wear long-sleeved shirts and long
       pants to avoid direct contact with insects, make sure you check for ticks daily, especially in unsuspecting areas of your body.

      ◈ Practice Campfire Safety

       Fires within your campground site should be at least 15 feet from tent walls, shrubs and trees, you can't leave the campfire unattended.
       Always keep a water bucket nearby and put the fire out before leaving — making sure to drown all the embers, not just the red ones.

      ◈ Watch Out For Wildlife

       Storing your food in a car, a bear-safe container or food storage locker can prevent attracting unwanted wildlife, do not feed them!
       If you come into contact with wild animals, make sure you wash your hands with disinfectant water and check your body for parasite.

Lake Camping

      Campsite Popularity ➔ ★ ★ ★ ★ ★
      ◈ Pay Attention To Diurnal Temperature Change

       The temperature difference between day and night of the lake is large, so make sure to keep warm, Change heavy clothes, prepare
       warm sleeping bags and Keep the campfire burning take all measures to keep warm.

      ◈ Stay Up-To-Date With The Weather

       Make sure you keep an eye on the weather, the lake is open and there is almost no place to avoid the bad weather. Make sure you have
       enough time to prepare for the possible bad weather.(such as rainstorm, hail, etc.)

      ◈ Make Sure Safety When Swimming

       Swimming is an interesting activity to camp at the lakeside, but unknown underwater environments can bring dangers, such as deep
       underwater depths and dangerous environmental organisms. Make sure to wear swimming equipment when swimmming.

Beach Camping

      Campsite Popularity ➔ ★ ★ ★ ★ ★
      ◈ Protect Your Skin From The Sun

       We often think UV rays are absent on cloudy days, but they can burn your skin just as severely as on a sunny day. Midday hours are
       when the sun’s rays are the strongest-seeking shade, wearing a hat or putting on sunglasses can help protect you from the UV rays.
       So we recommend using a broad-spectrum sunscreen and lip screen with at least SPF 15.

      ◈ Choose The Right Place To Set Up Your Tent

       When camping at the seaside, it is very important to choose the right location. Do not set up the tent too close to the sea, the change
       of tide may put you in danger.

      ◈ Leave your tent closed

       We always try and leave our tents shut when camping on the beach. Little grains of sand will blow past your tent all day. If you don’t
       shut your tent up properly, expect to be sharing your bed with a fine layer of sand!

Mountain Camping

      Campsite Popularity ➔ ★ ★ ★
      ◈ Adjust Your Backpack

       Wearing your backpack wrong exerts more pressure on your neck, shoulders, and back. So make sure to adjust your shoulder straps
       so that the backpack is high on your back and the straps are comfortable on your shoulders. A properly fitted backpack will keep you
       moving comfortably, even with a heavy load.


      ◈ Bring Snacks And Load Up On Water

       The secret to staying healthy in high altitude areas, especially if you love to hike on your camping trips is to drink a lot of water to
       stay hydrated and ensure your electrolytes remain balanced throughout. In this case, water and food that is high in potassium such
       as bananas are highly recommended. These will help ward off headaches and the muscle cramps that plaque most mountain campers.

Desert Camping

      Campsite popularity ➔ ★ ★
      ◈ Wear Layers

       Weather changes on a dime in the desert. A sweet, warm day quickly turns into a frozen wasteland at night. Temperature swings can vary
       up to 30 degrees a day in the desert so be sure to pack accordingly and layer up.


      ◈ Camp At Previously Established Campsites

       Even if you are a professional, I still highly recommend to camp at previously established campsites.
       There is a reason the campsites were established there, and doing this ensures your safety as there will be other people with you, making
       the chance of things going wrong much lower. In addition, it is always good to know your surroundings and where things are located
       around you, however, remember, never do this exploring alone.
